# Manifesto

Printers are not bad because printing is impossible. Printers are bad because the dominant business model rewards lock-in, confusion, planned friction, and consumable capture.

OpenFrame is the opposite bet:

- local-first printing
- repairable mechanics
- documented electronics
- visible error states
- replaceable modules
- published service procedures
- no cloud account requirement
- no consumable authentication lockout
- no fake mystery around rollers, fusers, drums, sensors, or network setup

The first product is deliberately boring: a monochrome office printer. The goal is not to win photo quality. The goal is to create the printer people can deploy, repair, trust, and explain.

The printer market has a hole exactly where Framework found one in laptops: not everyone needs magic. A lot of people just want hardware that respects them.
